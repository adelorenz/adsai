PhD PROGRAM

Why a PhD in applied Data Science and Artificial Intelligence?

The aim of this new PhD programme at UNITS is to provide students with the modern tools of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence, preparing them to create new data analysis methods and giving them the expertise to apply cutting-edge tools to problems in the real world. Our research spans from theoretical to applied aspects of data science and machine learning, aiming at understanding how modern methodologies became popular, framing their limitations and highlighting their possibility of extension.

After 3 years, our students will master modern concepts of machine learning and statistical analysis, corroborated by strong analytical problem solving skills. Graduates for instance will be trained in deep learning and neural networks models, in Bayesian methods and sampling algorithms, and in probabilistic programming, high performance computing and software development. At the end, students will be proficient in developing, from beginning to end, a complete data science solution to a complex real-world problem. Training in core disciplines will be complemented with the possibility to attend modules focusing on ethical aspects of data analysis, and the impact of technological development on regulations and society.

Graduates from our programme will be attractive for top-tier data science companies, and will have profiles ready to enter the academic world, in Italy and abroad. Given the broad impact of data science in modern society, industry and business, graduates will have the opportunity for a fulfilling career in a variety of fields. Potential careers paths range from the financial sector to digital services, healthcare, technology companies, market research and many others.

What’s the structure of the PhD programme?

The Applied Data Science and Artificial Intelligence (ADSAI) PhD program started with the 2021 cohort.

The PhD programme lasts for 3 years, and it is structured in two parts: the first year is mostly in the form of taught modules, which lay the theoretical and practical foundations for the knowledge base that students will draw upon in their research.

Starting approximately 9 months after their enrolment, students undertake their PhD research work in one of the subjects offered across SISSA, with at least one supervisor from the Theoretical and Scientific Data Science (TSDS) group, and often a co-supervisor from another group - or another institute altogether. We strongly encourage cross-disciplinary projects, offering the opportunity to pursue both a data science and a discipline-specific question. Fields range from astronomy to neurosciences, computer science to theory of neural networks, information theory to statistical physics, and many more. On occasion, some projects might involve the opportunity of a research placement in industry. All students will be encouraged and supported to present their work at international conferences and meetings at the appropriate point in time of their studies.

Our faculty is composed of a highly cross-disciplinary group of experts from the University of Trieste, which tightly interact with colleagues at The International School for Advanced Studies (SISSA) and the International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP).

Since 2021, ADSAI is also part of the Italian National PhD in AI.

We also teach and supervise Master final year projects for students enrolled in the Data Science and Scientific Computing Master of Science program at the University of Trieste. If you are interested in such a project, please get in touch with the relevant faculty member.
